Bioshock is a first person shooter that only allows for single player. This game really takes you for a ride though because you feel like shooting things is only the means to a greater end. You do not kill because you want to, you kill because you have to in order to progress in the story. Because the game puts such a heavy emphasis on taking the player on an adventure where everything is out to kill you, it achieves a perfect balance between action and narrative.

The story itself is the stuff of legend. You never know and cannot expect what will unfold next and it leaves you wondering what lies behind the next corner. After you've went around that corner though, you're left basking in the ingenuity of what just happened. On top of that surreal story-telling it doesn't feel at all forced, but the narrative progresses at a very natural pace.

The game mechanics are also a wonderful aspect of the game. The controls never feel awkward, but rather natural. You have enough combat options to go through the game guns blazing, or you can burn, shock and whack things to death. There is also an aspect of moral choice in the game, which really changes how fast you can grow stronger and what people say about you.
