I recently played through Bioshock from 2K Games, Feral Interactive and Irrational Games on the PC, and I can honestly say that it was a wonderful experience all the way through. Even though it is a fantastic game, this definitely is not a game for kids due to its mature rating with ESRB. Overall, Bioshock had wonderful balance, a great story and fantastic game mechanics.

Bioshock is a first person shooter that only allows for single player. This game really takes you for a ride though because you feel like shooting things is only the means to a greater end. You do not kill because you want to, you kill because you have to in order to progress in the story. Because the game puts such a heavy emphasis on taking the player on an adventure where everything is out to kill you, it achieves a perfect balance between action and narrative.

The story itself is the stuff of legend. You never know and cannot expect what will unfold next and it leaves you wondering what lies behind the next corner. After you've went around that corner though, you're left basking in the ingenuity of what just happened. On top of that surreal story-telling it doesn't feel at all forced, but the narrative progresses at a very natural pace.

The game mechanics are also a wonderful aspect of the game. The controls never feel awkward, but rather natural. You have enough combat options to go through the game guns blazing, or you can burn, shock and whack things to death. There is also an aspect of moral choice in the game, which really changes how fast you can grow stronger and what people say about you.

On the other hand, I found that the gameplay was a little on the easy side. Sure, I could have played the game on hard mode, but I feel that the normal mode should have provided more of a challenge because it was generally quite easy to get through. The greatest difficulty in Bioshock was having enough ammo to get by, often I ran out but that may be because I'm a bad shot. They really improved the ammo management in the second game however, which was great. Also, the ending was a little anticlimatic, I was expecting some sort of big interactive ending that really went out with a bang, but there was just a little narrative at the end. The ending was good, but it could have been much better.

The Final Verdict:

In general, with everything about the game in mind, I would give Bioshock an overwhelming 10 out of 10. It was truly a fanstastic experience.
